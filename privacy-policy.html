Cookies: We also use cookies in the Website. Cookies are small files that your browser stores on your computer or other device. A cookie allows the Website to recognize if you have visited the Website before and can store the user’s preferences and other information. For example, cookies can be used to collect information about your use of the Website during your current session (including the pages you visit and the files you download), the operating system, and the browser type of your computer or other device, your Internet service provider, your domain name and IP address and your general geographical location. If you are concerned about having cookies on your computer or device, you can set your browser to reject all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being set, which allows you to decide whether to accept them. You can also delete cookies from your computer or device. However, if you choose to block or delete cookies, certain features of the Website may not work properly.

Web Beacons: We can also use an application known as “web beacon”. A web beacon is an electronic file that generally consists of a single pixel image. It can be embedded in a web page or in an email to transmit information, which could include Personal Information. For example, it allows an email sender to determine if a user has opened a particular email.

Third-party tracking and behavioral-based advertising: We may also associate with certain third parties to collect, analyze and use part of the Personal and Non-Personal Information described in this section.
